Brand Guidelines

Do it pho love.

Visual identity & voice · Pho Love · Altoona, PA
01

Brand essence

A family's love, served in a bowl. Pho Love is warm, heartfelt, and unpretentious — a father's recipe, a son's homecoming, food made with love. The heart isn't decoration. It's the brand.

02

Logo

The primary lockup is the bowl-and-heart mark with the wordmark and "Vietnamese Restaurant." Use the full-color version on light surfaces and the reversed version on dark or photographic backgrounds.

Primary · on light / cream
Reversed · on ink / dark
Submark · profile pic, favicon, to-go stamp

Do

  • Keep clear space around the logo — at least the height of the bowl
  • Use the reversed version on dark or busy backgrounds
  • Scale the whole lockup proportionally
  • Let the gradient and the heart lead the brand

Don't

  • Stretch, squash, or rotate the logo
  • Recolor the bowl or drop the heart
  • Place the dark logo on a dark or red background
  • Add shadows, outlines, or effects
03

04

Typography

The wordmark uses a hand-brushed script (built into the logo). For everything else, a friendly two-font system.

05

The heart motif

The heart shows up in the logo bowl, in "Made with Love," in the "<3" they sign off with. Make it the thread that ties everything together — a divider, a bullet, a stamp on the to-go bag.

♥Heart bullets in menus ♥Section dividers ♥"Made with love" stamp
06

Voice & tone

Warm, loving, casual, family-first. First person — "our broth," "we love you." Honest and human, never salesy.

Sounds like

"10+ hours of love in every bowl."

"Come hungry, leave family."

"Made with love, served with more."

Not like

"An authentic culinary experience."

Corporate, hype, or generic restaurant-speak.

Anything that sounds like a chain.

Ready-to-post captions

"10+ hours of love simmering before we even open. ♥"

"A father's recipe, a son's homecoming. Come hungry, leave family."

"New week, same slow broth. Pull up to 220 Park Hills Plaza. ♥"
